NORTH AMERICA

Canada

Food: Canadians eat breakfast around 8 am, lunch around noon, and dinner around 6pm. Each meal is complemented by tea, coffee, or snacks. Manners include keepign both hands above the table during a meal.

The Arts and Music: Art galleries and museums are popular. Art focuses on Canadian landscape. Popular crafts include pottery and quilting. Montreal's jazz, comedy, and film festivals are popular attractions. Native American tribes are reviving traditional arts, including clothing decorated with paint, beads, and porcupine quills; jewelry; leatherwork; and woodwork

History: Early native people dominated Canada before Vikings and, later, French settled in North America. The vast area has retained ties with Britain and it's French roots.

Fun Facts: Canada borders three oceans: the Atlantic, the Pacific, and the Arctic. Most of Canada's population lives within 100 miles of the US border. In March 2012, the Canadian government stopped making and distributing pennies. Canada stretches across six time zones. Canada is the second largest country in the world.
