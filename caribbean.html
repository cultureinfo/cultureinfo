Dominican Republic

Food:

Dominican Republic is known for its variety of delicious foods.Some of Dominican Republic'c main dishes are mungu, which is smashed plantain, with salami,eggs, fried cheese, onions, and avocado. There's also chimichuri, which is a hamburger with cabbage,and tomatoes on italian bread. There's also sancocho, which is a soup made of beef, chicken, corn, plantains, potatoe, and can be eaten with a bowl of rice and a piece of avoacado.


Music:

The Dominican Republic has a diversity of different type songs like merengue, batchata, and dembow. Merengue is is a style of Dominican music and dance. Partners hold each other in a closed position. The leader holds the follower's waist with the leader's right hand, while holding the follower's right hand with the leader's left hand at the follower's eye level. Bachata is a style of dance that originated in the Dominican Republic. It is danced widely all over the world but not identically. The basics to the dance are three-step with a Cuban hip motion, followed by a tap including a hip movement on the 4th beat. The main element of dembow music is its rhythm, which is somewhat reminiscent of reggaeton and dancehall music, but with a more constant rhythm and faster than reggaeton.

Puerto Rico

Food:

Some of famous dishes in Puerto Rico are lechon which is pork cooked over carbon, there's also escabeche which is marinated fish, and lastly there's cuchifrito, made of yellow rice with red beans, olives, plantains and pork.


Music:

Puerto Rico has a diverse range of music like danza and bolero. Danza is a musical genre that originated in Ponce, a city in southern Puerto Rico. It is a popular turn-of-the-twentieth-century ballroom dance genre slightly similar to the waltz. Bolero is a genre of slow-tempo Latin music and its associated dance.

Cuba

Food:

Some examples of cuban foods are the Cuban Sandwhich, which has pickles, beef, ham, and cheese. There's also ropa vieja, when translated to english it means old clothe, it may not sound appatizing but it is actually stewed beef in chili, tomato, onion, and cumin sauce.


Music:

Popular Cuban music are salsa and Timba. Salsa represents a mix of Latin musical genres, but its primary component is Cuban dance music. The roots of salsa originated in Eastern Cuba from the Cuban Son and Afro-Cuban dance. Timba Timba is a Cuban genre of music based on popular Cuban music along with salsa, American funk/R&B, and the strong influence of Afro-Cuban folkloric music.


Fun Facts:

The official name for Cuba is the Republic of Cuba, Cuba is the largest island in the Carribean, Cuba is often reffered to as, "El Cocodrilo" or "El Caima" since the island is shaped as a crocodile, Cuba has a population of 11 million people, and sugar is the main crop in Cuba.


History:

The island of Cuba was inhabited by various Mesoamerican cultures prior to the arrival of the Italian explorer Christopher Columbus in 1492. After Columbus' arrival, Cuba became a Spanish colony, ruled by a Spanish governor in Havana.

Bahamas

Food:

Famous dishes in the Bahamas are JohnnyCakes, pigeons peas and rice, and rock lobster. Johnnycake is a cornmeal flatbread, pigeons peas and rice consists of rice, tomato, oregano, cumin, salt, achiote, and pigeon peas. Rock lobster is self explanatory based on the name and it is baked lobster with vegetables.


Music:

Goombay Music with Rake and Scrape Bands. Historically, secular music in The Bahamas has been called goombay music, a derivative of the Gambian word gumbay, or large drum. Bahamian secular music, particularly when played by rake and scrape bands, relies on the goatskin drum to create its rhythmic base.


Fun Facts:

Some fun facts about the Bahamas are The word Bahamas is derived from a Spanish word 'baja mar' which means 'low tide' or 'shallow water or sea'. The Bahamas were once a favorite place for pirates to hide their treasure. The Bahamas encompasses an area of 5,358 square miles.


History:

The Arawak Indians were the first inhabitants of the Bahamas. Columbus's first encounter with the New World was on Oct. 12, 1492, when he landed on the Bahamian island of San Salvador. The British first built settlements on the islands in the 17th century.
